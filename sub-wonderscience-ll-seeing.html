Seeing

You use light to see the world around you! How does this work?

Suppose you are standing outside, looking at the green leaves of a tree. Light made inside the sun travels all the way from the sun to the leaves of the tree. This sunlight is made up of all colours of light, but when it bounces off the leaves it is mainly the green light which comes out. This is why leaves look green!

The green light that bounces off the leaves then travels to your eye. There is a lens in the front of your eye which makes a picture of the tree on the back of your eye. This picture is then detected by your brain. This is how light is used to see the world around you!
